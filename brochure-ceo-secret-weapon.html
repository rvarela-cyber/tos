When Two Giants
Become One.
Confidential | Texas Financial Sector | Downtown Dallas | $16M Project Scope

In the mid-1980s, two of the largest Texas-owned banks in the state merged to form a single institution. The decision was made in a boardroom. The execution landed on Move Solutions.

The challenge was not the scope alone -- at $16 million it was the largest commercial relocation in Texas at the time. The challenge was that two entirely different organizations had to become operationally unified without losing a single business day.

Move Solutions deployed a phased relocation strategy that sequenced entire departments in coordinated overnight waves -- each team moved after close and fully operational by open. Custom work orders were written for every floor. Elevator access was negotiated with building management weeks in advance. Every piece of furniture, equipment, and file was tracked to its new address before the first truck was loaded.

What this project proved -- and what has been proven 57,000 times since -- is that when the stakes are highest, process is the only thing that does not fail.

A regional healthcare network needed to migrate its primary data center to a co-location facility across the metro. Their lease was expiring and their current facility was losing power redundancy. TechTeam Solutions accepted the constraint and built the entire project around it. At hour 71, the last system verification was signed off. Patient record systems came back online with no access interruptions. The healthcare network's CIO said it was the first time a vendor had delivered exactly what they promised on a timeline he had privately believed was impossible.
