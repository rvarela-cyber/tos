A Crate That Changed The Industry

We Engineered Our Way Out Of The Cardboard Problem.

Commercial moving ran on cardboard for decades. Single-use boxes assembled, packed, loaded, and crushed into a dumpster within days. Across thousands of office relocations, that volume adds up to a staggering amount of landfill material.

Move Solutions developed the Stack-N-Pack, No-Lift Crate as a direct response to that problem. Every crate is crush-proof, secure, and stackable. Each one is RFID-tagged so it gets recovered, cleaned, and recirculated after every job. One crate serves hundreds of moves before it ever needs to be replaced.

Inside Our Own Four Walls

How We Run Our Operations.

The same standards we apply on client projects run inside our own facilities. These practices aren't marketing language. They're how we manage equipment, energy, and assets day to day.

Energy-Aware Workstations.

Application monitoring tracks resource consumption across our systems and powers down equipment when it goes idle. We're not running machines overnight on jobs that finished in the afternoon.

Responsible End-Of-Life Computing.

When our own hardware reaches retirement, it goes through the same certified channel we use for client equipment. CPUs and monitors are disassembled and processed. Nothing in our own fleet quietly ends up in a skip.

RFID Crate Recovery System.

Every crate in our 150,000-unit fleet carries an RFID tag. That tracking system is what allows us to recover units from client sites rather than writing them off as lost. Fewer replacements needed means less new plastic produced.

Common Questions

What Most Clients Ask About Sustainability.

How do you handle leftover items after a move or decommission?
It depends on the condition and category of material. Furniture and equipment in working condition typically gets donated or resold. Items without a reuse path go to recycling streams sorted by material type. Only what genuinely cannot be diverted ends up in landfill, and we document the breakdown in your project closeout so you know exactly what went where.
Do I get to decide which organization receives donated items?
Yes. You name the recipient, we coordinate the delivery and documentation. If you'd like a suggestion, we have working relationships with regional nonprofits that regularly accept commercial office furniture and equipment in various conditions. Either way, you receive written confirmation of the donation for your records.
What certifications cover your data destruction and e-waste services?
TechTeam Solutions, the data and technology arm of our family of companies, holds NAID AAA Certification for hard drive destruction and R2 Certification for electronics recycling. Every project that touches sensitive media results in a Certificate of Destruction and Recycling documenting what was processed, how, and where materials went afterward.
How much material actually gets kept out of the landfill?
The exact figure varies by project, but on-site compaction alone cuts landfill volume by up to 50%. Layer in the donation and recycling channels and the majority of material from a typical commercial relocation or decommission never reaches disposal. Diversion rates are tracked and can be included in your project closeout documentation.
Why are reusable crates genuinely better than cardboard boxes?
A single crate handles hundreds of projects across its lifetime. A cardboard box gets one move, sometimes two. Beyond the environmental math, reusable crates are crush-proof, lockable, and designed to stack without shifting, which means less product damage and faster loading. The environmental benefit and the operational benefit point in the same direction.
Does a sustainability-focused approach cost more?
Generally, no. The crate system, the recycling infrastructure, and the donation coordination are part of how we operate, not premium add-ons. In decommission work especially, resale and donation value from exiting assets can offset a portion of the project cost. When there are incremental costs for certified destruction or specialty recycling, those are line-itemed clearly in your proposal so there are no surprises.
Can you support clients with formal ESG or sustainability reporting requirements?
Yes. A substantial share of our work runs through enterprise accounts and national clients with active ESG programs. We coordinate with internal sustainability teams, procurement, and facilities to produce the diversion data, donation documentation, and chain-of-custody records those programs require. Share your reporting framework early and we'll structure deliverables to match.
What happens to materials that can't be recycled through standard channels?
For materials outside standard municipal or commercial recycling, we work through certified regional specialists who handle those streams. Foam, certain composites, and specialty electronics often need processing that goes beyond what local channels accept. If a material has no viable recovery path, that's documented in the project closeout, so the diversion data we report is accurate rather than inflated.
Can Move Solutions provide sustainability documentation for our corporate records?
Yes. Project documentation at close can include material diversion rates by category, donation recipient confirmation, recycling stream summaries, and Certificates of Destruction for any data-bearing media. If your team uses a specific reporting template, share it during project planning and we'll align our deliverables to fit it.
