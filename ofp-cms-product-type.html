Three-screen workflow.

Screen 1 (this screen) — Consignor Information. The user picks Add New Consignor (shown active) or Edit Existing Consignors. For a new consignor, they fill in:

  • Consignor Contact — Contact Name and Company (required); Email and Phone (recommended).
  • Revenue Share — Split Structure dropdown with presets (50/50, 60/40, 70/30, 40/60, 30/70) plus a Custom split option that opens two percent fields with a live sum check.
  • Account & Agreement — Consignor Portal Access toggle (read-only login) and Signed Agreement PDF upload (master contract).

Clicking Save Consignor writes the consignor to the system and routes the user to Screen 2. (You can click the button above to preview Screen 2.)

Screen 2 — Acknowledgement. A simple confirmation that the consignor was saved successfully, with a single Add Product button (and a secondary link to return to consignor list).

Screen 3 — Add Product (standard WooCommerce Add New Product page). The user lands on the normal product entry screen — product title, description, image gallery, categories, brands, publish box — with the Product Data box pre-set to Consignment. The Consignment tab in Product Data exposes the per-product attributes (Pricing, Availability & Lifecycle, Auto-Markdown, Internal Notes) the developer must add into that box. The consignor link is already attached behind the scenes from Screen 1.

Consignor Report — does not live on any of these three screens. Conner runs it from a separate Consignor Reports page in the dashboard, where he picks a consignor and pulls every product tied to them (active, sold, returned) with quantities, list prices, sale dates, and split totals owed. Exports to CSV or PDF.
